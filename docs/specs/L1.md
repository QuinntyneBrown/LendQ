# LendQ — L1 High-Level Requirements

## L1-1: User Authentication & Secure Session Management
The system shall provide login, sign-up, forgot-password, email verification, and secure session management flows using email-based identity and password authentication. Sessions shall support secure renewal, logout, device/session revocation, and forced invalidation after sensitive account changes.

## L1-2: User Management & RBAC
The system shall provide administrative screens for managing users, roles, permissions, and account status. Roles include at minimum: Admin, Creditor, and Borrower. A single user may hold multiple roles simultaneously. Role, permission, and account-status changes shall be audit logged and shall take effect immediately for new and existing sessions.

## L1-3: Loan Management & Terms Governance
The system shall allow creditors to create, view, edit, and manage loans. Each loan includes a principal amount, description, borrower, currency, repayment terms (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly, or custom), interest rate (optional), installment count or maturity date, and status. Creditors can update any field on their loans. Borrowers can view their loans and interact with borrower-allowed fields and actions only. Principal and creditor-controlled contractual terms shall not be directly editable by borrowers without creditor approval.

## L1-4: Payment Tracking, Scheduling & Balance Management
The system shall track scheduled obligations and actual payment transactions against a loan, show next payment due, payment history, payment allocations, and outstanding balance. Borrowers can make payments, including lump-sum and partial payments, and can initiate allowed schedule changes. Creditors can adjust payment details according to their role. The system shall preserve an immutable audit trail of payments, reversals, schedule changes, and balance-affecting events.

## L1-5: Dashboard & Overview
The system shall provide a dashboard showing a summary of all lending activity for the logged-in user: money lent out, money owed, upcoming payments, overdue payments, and total outstanding balances across all loans, both as creditor and borrower.

## L1-6: Notifications & Alerts
The system shall provide in-app notifications and optional email alerts for payment due dates, overdue payments, payment received confirmations, loan modifications, and schedule changes. Notification delivery shall be near real time for active sessions, deduplicated, retryable, and trackable.

## L1-7: Responsive Design
All screens shall be designed with responsive layouts supporting mobile (375px), tablet (768px), and desktop (1280px+) viewports. All buttons, dialogs, notifications, forms, navigation, and data views shall adapt to each breakpoint.

## L1-8: Application Security & Abuse Protection
The system shall enforce strong security controls appropriate for a financial application, including secure session handling, credential protection, rate limiting, anti-automation measures, secure headers, and protection against unauthorized access or replay of sensitive operations.

## L1-9: Financial Integrity & Auditability
The system shall maintain an authoritative, consistent, and auditable financial record. Loan terms, schedules, payments, reversals, and balance calculations shall be historically traceable, versioned or logged, and reproducible from the system of record.

## L1-10: Accessibility, Client Quality & Performance
The system shall provide accessible user interfaces, clear loading, error, empty, and success states, role-aware navigation, and performant client experiences on supported devices and viewports.

## L1-11: Reliability, Observability & Operational Readiness
The system shall provide reliable background processing, structured logging, metrics, tracing, health checks, backup and restore procedures, and automated quality gates required for secure production deployment.

## L1-12: API Contract & Integration Governance
The system shall provide a versioned, consistent, machine-readable API contract with stable error semantics and safe retry behavior for clients and internal integrations.

## L1-13: Bank Accounts & Fund Management
The system shall allow users to have bank accounts within the platform. Admin users can deposit and withdraw funds to/from bank accounts. Users and admins can set up recurring deposits (scheduled payments) into bank accounts. Bank account balances, transaction history, and recurring deposit schedules shall be visible and manageable.

## L1-14: Savings Goals
The system shall allow users to create and manage savings goals. Each goal has a target amount, optional deadline, and tracks progress toward the target. Users can allocate funds from their bank account toward savings goals, view progress, and mark goals as complete.

## L1-15: Recurring Loans
The system shall allow creditors to set up recurring loans that automatically create new loan instances on a defined schedule (e.g., monthly allowance loans). Each recurrence generates a new loan with the same terms, borrower, and repayment structure. Creditors can pause, resume, or cancel the recurrence.
